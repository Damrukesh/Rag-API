Your fear is very common, but the reality is much better than you're imagining.

First: No, you are not "stuck" after joining Infosys

If you join in September 2026:

Sept 2026 → Training
Around Mar 2027 → Project allocation
By Sept 2027 → ~1 year experience

You can switch at 3 different stages:

During training (off-campus opportunities)
After 6–12 months experience
After 1.5–2 years experience

Many people move from Infosys/TCS/Wipro to 12–25 LPA companies within 1–2 years.

Second: You are no longer competing with 2026 freshers

This is actually a good thing.

After 1 year:

You are competing for:

Associate Software Engineer
Software Development Engineer
ML Engineer
Data Engineer
AI Engineer

instead of fresher campus hiring.

Companies care about:

Skills
Projects
Interview performance

far more than your original college placement.

The real danger

The danger is not Infosys.

The danger is spending 1 year doing only:

Internal training
Mandatory project work
No DSA
No portfolio
No networking

Then suddenly trying to switch.

That is where people get trapped.

If I were in your position

I would divide the next 18 months like this:

Phase 1: Now → Joining (2 months)

Focus only on:

DSA
Python
SQL
One strong AI/ML project

Do not scatter into 10 different technologies.

Phase 2: Training Period (6 months)

Spend 1-2 hours daily on:

LeetCode
System Design basics
Cloud (AWS/Azure)
Git/GitHub
AI/ML project portfolio

Goal:
Have a profile stronger than a typical Infosys trainee.

Phase 3: First 6-12 months after allocation

Choose one target:

Path A: SDE

DSA
Backend
System Design

Path B: AI/ML Engineer (matches your long-term goal)

ML
LLMs
MLOps
Cloud deployment
Production AI projects

Don't try to master both simultaneously.

About the 15 LPA target

15 LPA is not unrealistic at all.

What usually gets people there:

Strong DSA
Good GitHub
2-3 serious projects
Referral-based applications
Consistent interview preparation

Not:

Infosys brand name
Years of experience
What I would optimize for

Given your earlier goal of building an AI/ML career and eventually keeping options open for higher studies abroad, I would use Infosys as:

Salary + experience source

while spending the next 12-18 months building:

AI/ML portfolio
LeetCode skills
Cloud skills
Professional GitHub profile

Think of Infosys as a launchpad, not your destination.

If you execute consistently, a move from Infosys to a 12–20 LPA role within 1–2 years is a very realistic outcome. The people who struggle are usually the ones who stop learning after joining.
